Take Care of Your Mind

College life can be draining, it is normal to feel stressed or overwhelmed. Between classes and personal life, your mental wellbeing can get ignored. This space is a reminder that your mental health matters, small steps make big differences.

Common Challenges

  • Anxiety related to academic preformace
  • Stress from academic pressure
  • Burnout from constant workload
  • Sleep problems caused by irregular schedules
  • Loneliness or isolation

Ways to Cope

Mindfulness and Meditation

This helps students slow down and manage stress, it reduces anxiety, improves focus, and supports emotions. Activities like deep breathing and yoga can greatly improve how you cope with daily challenges.

Set Goals and Routines

Setting small realistic goals and maintaining simple routines can help students feel in control. Having a routine provides structure during stressful times and achieving goals makes tasks feel less overwhelming.

Connect with Others

Friends and family help combat the feeling of isolation. Building these connections helps students feel supported and reminds them that they are not alone. Try joining a club around campus.

Student Resources

  • Student Counseling Center
  • Student Health Center
  • Student Wellness Center
  • Center for Students in Recovery

Area Mental Health Facilites

  • North Texas Behavioral Health Authority (NTBHA)
  • LifePath Systems

Growth doesn't happen all at once, it's okay to move at your own pace. College can be defeating, but struggling doesn't mean you're failing. Give yourself grace to take breaks, be patient with yourself, and don't hesitate to reach out for support. Your productivity does not define your worth.
